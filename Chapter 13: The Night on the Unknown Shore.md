# Chapter 13: The Night on the Unknown Shore

The boat finally came to rest near the quiet shore of the island. The waves moved gently now, as if the ocean itself had calmed after revealing its secret. Joseph dropped the anchor.

“This should be safe for the night,” he said.

The sky was slowly turning dark. Stars began to appear one by one. The island stood silent, waiting. They set up a small camp near the shore. Sharan and Keerthi quickly brought out the fish they had caught earlier that day.

“Good thing we didn’t eat these before,” Keerthi said with a smile.

Harish laughed softly. “Feels like we were saving them for this moment.”

Soon, a small fire was lit. The warm glow of the flames danced across their faces. Harish and Keerthi sat near the fire, carefully cleaning and preparing the fish. The sound of the waves mixed with the crackling of the fire—a peaceful moment after everything they had been through.

Inside the tent, Abinaya sat quietly. The Kadalkol book rested in her hands. She slowly opened it. The pages were old, yet perfectly preserved. Her eyes moved carefully across the lines. Some words were simple, clear, and understandable. But many parts were strange. Certain lines were underlined. Symbols repeated. Phrases did not fully make sense at first glance.

Abinaya leaned closer. “This isn’t just a book…” she whispered to herself. “It’s a puzzle.”

Her mind began connecting the patterns. King Athiyan had not written this like a normal record. This book was meant for someone specific: for Mathi. Every line carried hidden meaning—riddles, logic, and emotions hidden between words. Only they both could truly understand it. But Abinaya was patient, sharp, and curious. Slowly, she began to understand parts of it. Not everything, but enough to know that this book held secrets far deeper than history.

She reached for the rolled scroll beside her. Her fingers gently touched it. She was about to open it when Keerthi called from outside, “Abinaya! We need help here!”

Abinaya quickly closed the book. “Coming!” She placed the scroll safely beside the bag and stepped out of the tent.

Meanwhile, Joseph and Sharan picked up a lantern. “Let’s take a quick look around,” Joseph said. Sharan nodded, carrying his small detection machine and notebook. “Just a short distance.”

They stepped into the forest. The trees were tall and dense. The air felt different—still. The lantern light barely cut through the darkness. Leaves rustled softly with the wind. They walked slowly, careful with each step. Sharan kept noting their path: distance from shore, direction, and landmarks.

Joseph looked around quietly. “We shouldn’t go too far.”

After walking a short distance, they both suddenly stopped. In front of them stood a massive stone wall, about ten to twelve feet high. Not too tall, but long—very long. It stretched across the forest, disappearing into the darkness on both sides, covered in age and partly buried in sand and roots.

Joseph stepped closer, raising the lantern. “This… is not natural.”

Sharan placed his hand on the stone surface. “It’s man-made.” He paused, studying it carefully. “The height is interesting…”

Joseph looked at him. “What do you mean?”

Sharan ran his fingers along the edge. “It’s not built like a defensive wall.”

Joseph frowned. “Too short for protection?”

Sharan nodded slowly. “Yes… if this was meant to keep enemies out, it would be much taller.” He stepped back, observing the length. “This feels more like a boundary.”

Joseph’s eyes narrowed. “A marked region?”

Sharan quickly noted it down. “Or something that was once taller… and now partially buried under sand.”

Joseph looked around the forest again. “If part of it is under the ground, then this island is hiding more than what we see.”

A quiet realization settled between them. This was not just land; it was buried history. Sharan placed his detection machine on the ground for a moment. Suddenly—*Beep… Beep… Beep…*

The machine came alive. Both of them looked down. “It detected something,” Sharan said. Joseph quickly knelt and brushed away the sand. Something metallic reflected in the lantern light. Carefully, they dug it out. 

A small object emerged—round, heavy, and golden. Joseph lifted it into the light. A gold coin, shining brightly. Sharan checked it quickly. “About 4.3 grams…”

Joseph examined the markings. “This matches…”

Sharan completed the thought. “Chola dynasty gold Gadyana coins.”

For a moment, silence. Then Joseph smiled. “We’re not just exploring anymore.”

Sharan nodded. “We’ve found proof.”

Without wasting another second, they ran back toward the shore. At the camp, the others were sitting near the fire when they heard footsteps. Joseph and Sharan rushed in. “Look at this!” Sharan said, slightly out of breath. 

He placed the coin in Harish’s hand. The firelight reflected on its surface. Everyone leaned closer. Keerthi gasped. “It’s real…”

Abinaya looked at the carvings carefully. Her eyes filled with wonder. “This is from the Chola period…”

Joseph nodded. “And that’s not all.” He looked at Harish. “There’s a massive stone wall inside the forest. Still standing.”

Harish’s expression changed. “That means…”

Abinaya finished softly. “This island was once inhabited.”

A deep silence followed. Then slowly, smiles appeared. Not just excitement, but confidence. They were on the right path. The history they were chasing was real, and it was leading them somewhere for a reason.

Dinner that night felt different—warmer, full of quiet excitement. The fish tasted better than ever. The ocean breeze felt softer. Even the fire burned brighter. Later, they prepared to sleep. The night grew calm and peaceful, but filled with hidden possibilities.

Abinaya stood near the tent entrance for a moment. She looked at Harish. A gentle smile appeared on her face. “Good night,” she said softly.

Harish smiled back. “Good night.”

A little distance away, Sharan looked at Keerthi. “Good night,” he said quietly. Keerthi smiled warmly. “Good night, Sharan.”

One by one, they all settled into their tents. The fire slowly faded. The waves continued their rhythm. Above them, the stars watched silently. And deep within the island, hidden beneath time, something was waiting for morning.
