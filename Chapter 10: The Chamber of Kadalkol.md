# Chapter 10: The Chamber of Kadalkol

Deep inside the ancient temple, the silence felt heavy. Two paths. Two journeys. Both moving deeper into the forgotten world of King Athiyan. And somewhere ahead, the truth waited.

### Harish and Abinaya – The Trap Corridor

Harish and Abinaya stood frozen at the edge of the broken floor. The deep pit beneath them looked endless. Stone tiles continued across the corridor, but many of them were false steps. One wrong move and they would fall.

Abinaya slowly knelt near the edge. Her fingers brushed the stone tiles carefully. “These aren’t random traps,” she said quietly.

Harish looked closer. “What do you mean?”

Abinaya shone her light across the floor again. Some tiles had faint markings—small symbols carved into the stone. Harish leaned closer. “They look like letters.”

Abinaya’s eyes widened. “Ancient Tamil.”

She quickly took out the page she had copied from the Athiyan history book. Her mind raced. The symbols on the tiles formed parts of a phrase. Not a warning, but a message. 

Harish noticed something. “Look.” Three tiles in front of them had similar carvings.

Abinaya studied them carefully. Then she spoke slowly. “It’s a line from an ancient poem.”

Harish raised an eyebrow. “You’re sure?”

Abinaya nodded. “It was written during the time of the southern kingdoms.” She looked at the tiles again. “They form the phrase: *The path of truth stands firm.*”

Harish smiled slightly. “So the real tiles must follow the order of the words.”

Abinaya nodded. “Yes.” She pointed to the first correct symbol.

Harish stepped carefully onto it. The tile held. No movement. No collapse. “Good start,” he whispered.

Abinaya followed behind him, guiding each step carefully. One tile. Then another. Then another. Every correct symbol formed part of the poetic phrase. The ancient puzzle was guiding them safely across the corridor. Slowly, carefully, they crossed the deadly floor.

At the far end, Harish stepped onto solid ground again. He exhaled deeply. “That king really loved puzzles.”

Abinaya smiled. “Or he wanted only the wise to reach the truth.”

They continued forward into the deeper chamber.

### Joseph – The Guardians

Meanwhile, in the other corridor, Joseph moved slowly backward as the stone guardians approached. The massive statues had stepped down from their pedestals. Their glowing eyes followed him. Heavy stone footsteps echoed across the chamber.

Joseph quickly looked around. Running would not work. Fighting them would be impossible. They were too large and too strong. But ancient mechanisms always had weaknesses. 

Joseph studied the floor, then the statues, then the walls. And suddenly, he noticed something. The statues were not walking randomly. They were stepping on specific stone patterns. 

Joseph crouched slightly. Each statue followed the same square floor markings—a controlled movement path. Joseph smiled slightly. “So that’s how you work.”

He stepped sideways onto a tile that the statues had avoided. The nearest guardian suddenly stopped. Its glowing eyes flickered. Joseph stepped again onto another untouched tile. The statues froze completely. The glowing eyes dimmed. 

Then slowly, they returned to their original positions along the wall. Joseph let out a quiet breath. “Defense system… deactivated.”

He straightened and continued down the corridor. Moments later, a faint light appeared ahead.



Harish and Abinaya reached the end of their corridor at the same moment Joseph stepped into the chamber from the opposite side. The room was enormous—a circular hall supported by massive stone pillars. Ancient carvings covered every wall. 

At the center of the chamber stood a large stone pedestal. The three friends slowly approached it. On top of the pedestal rested a stone box wrapped in ancient cloth. Joseph brushed away the dust. The royal symbol of Athiyan was carved on the lid.

Abinaya’s eyes sparkled with excitement. “This must be it.”

Harish reached forward to lift the lid, but it did not move. Joseph frowned. “It’s locked.”

They examined the pedestal carefully. Then Abinaya noticed something unusual. “Wait.” 

She crouched lower and looked beneath the stone box. At the center of the pedestal was a circular hollow space carved into the stone. A perfectly shaped slot, as if something important was meant to be placed there.

Joseph studied the shape carefully. “This is a key mechanism.”

Harish nodded. “The box won’t open unless something fits into that slot.”

Abinaya ran her fingers along the carved edges. “The shape looks familiar…” She paused. Her mind raced back to something they had brought with them—something ancient, carved with care. Her eyes slowly lifted toward the small pouch hanging from her belt. 

Inside it rested the Maragatham gemstone the priest had given her before they left the village. Harish noticed the change in her expression. “You’re thinking the same thing, right?”

Abinaya nodded slowly. “The gemstone…”

Joseph looked at the pedestal again. “The shape matches.”

The three friends stared at the hollow slot beneath the box. The temple had been waiting for centuries for the one object that could unlock its secret. 

But before placing the gemstone, Joseph raised his hand. “Wait.”

The chamber around them was still silent—too silent. Ancient mechanisms had already tried to stop them, and this temple had protected its secrets for centuries. If the gemstone truly was the key, then placing it might awaken something else.

Harish looked at the pedestal. Abinaya held the pouch carefully in her hand. The moment had come. But what would happen next, none of them could yet know.
