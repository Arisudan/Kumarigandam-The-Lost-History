# Chapter 16: The Chamber of Secrets

The stone door opened fully, and the friends stepped inside. The room was small, quiet, and circular in shape. At the center stood a round stone table—simple, but powerful. The air inside felt different, heavier, as if the room itself was holding memories.

“Stay alert,” Joseph said softly.

Abinaya placed her bag carefully on the stone table. Harish and the others followed, and one by one, they set their belongings down. Then, slowly, they began to explore.

The walls of the chamber were covered with paintings. Not broken, not faded, but preserved, as if time had chosen to protect this place. Keerthi moved her torch across one wall. “These are… stories.”

Sharan nodded. “Not just stories… history.”

Each painting showed moments: a kingdom, people, festivals, war, love, and loss. Joseph stood still before one particular painting. His eyes widened. “This…” He stepped closer. “…this must be them.”

The others gathered around. There, painted with great detail, were two figures. A man—a king with strong, sharp eyes and a presence that commanded respect. And beside him, a woman—graceful and calm, her eyes filled with kindness.

Abinaya whispered softly, “Athiyan… and Mathi.”

Harish looked closely. “They look… real.”

Joseph nodded. “This is not imagination. These are real faces.”

Sharan quickly took out his device. “We need to document everything.” *Click. Click. Click.* Photos were taken and notes were written. Every detail mattered.

Meanwhile, back at the center of the room, something shifted. A soft sound, a small slip—the Maragatham gemstone fell from Abinaya’s bag. It rolled gently until it reached the center of the stone table. And then, it stopped.

For a brief second, nothing happened. Then, it began to glow. A soft green light, growing brighter and stronger. 

“Harish!” Abinaya called out.

Everyone turned, their eyes fixed on the table. The gemstone pulsed with light—alive, awakened. Joseph walked closer. “This is different…”

Sharan leaned in. “Something’s reacting.”

Harish quickly cleared the bags from the table. “Move everything.”

As the surface became visible, they saw it: a carved circular slot, perfectly shaped and exactly matching the gemstone. Abinaya took a deep breath. “This was meant for it.”

She gently picked up the Maragatham stone, held it for a moment, and then slowly placed it into the slot. The moment it fit—*click*. 

The table vibrated. A low sound echoed through the chamber. A hidden section in the table opened, sliding outward. Inside lay a scroll, old and carefully preserved. 

No one moved. Joseph spoke first. “Wait.” They all looked at him. “This could be another mechanism. If we take it… something might trigger.”

Silence. They all knew he could be right. Abinaya stood still, thinking fast. Then suddenly, her eyes widened. “The scroll…” she whispered.

Harish looked at her. “What?”

Abinaya quickly opened her bag. “I already have one.” She took out the scroll she had found earlier with the **Kadalkol** book—still unopened, still unknown. “I didn’t open it before… but now… it makes sense.”

The others gathered close. Slowly and carefully, she opened the scroll. The paper unfolded—old and fragile, but clear. It was a map with lines, markings, territory boundaries, and ancient symbols. 

Sharan leaned closer. “This is…”

“A region map,” Joseph completed.

Abinaya traced the lines with her finger. “But it’s incomplete…”

Everyone noticed it. Only half of the map was present. The other half was missing. Keerthi looked at the scroll inside the table, then back at Abinaya. “Don’t tell me…”

Harish finished the thought. “The second half is here.”

Silence filled the room. Two scrolls. Two halves. One complete truth. 

Joseph crossed his arms. “Or… a trap.”

Abinaya looked at both scrolls, her mind racing. “If Athiyan created this… he wouldn’t make it simple.”

Harish looked at her. “So what do we do?”

Abinaya took a deep breath, her eyes steady. “We think.”

The glowing gemstone remained fixed in the table, still shining and active. The unopened scroll lay in front of them, waiting. And somewhere beyond this moment, the truth of everything they had been searching for was just one decision away.

Is the second scroll truly the missing piece… or something far more dangerous?
