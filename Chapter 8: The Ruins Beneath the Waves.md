# Chapter 8: The Ruins Beneath the Waves

The boat moved steadily across the darkening sea. Wind brushed across the deck as the vessel cut through the open waters. The sky above had grown brighter, but the ocean here looked deeper and more mysterious than anywhere they had traveled before.

Joseph stood at the navigation console. His eyes were fixed on the coordinates flashing on the screen. “Almost there,” he said.

Sharan leaned over the sonar display beside him. Numbers scrolled slowly across the screen: depth readings, seafloor shapes, and signals bouncing from the ocean floor far below. Abinaya stood nearby holding the notebook where she had copied the ancient map references. She compared the numbers carefully. “Coordinates match,” she said quietly.

Harish stepped closer to the console. “How far now?”

Joseph looked at the GPS. “Less than half a nautical mile.”

The group fell silent. After centuries of silence… after maps, inscriptions, and hidden clues… they had finally reached the place. The boat slowed. Joseph gently reduced the engine power until the vessel drifted quietly above the marked point. The ocean around them stretched endlessly in every direction. No land. No ships. Just water.

Sharan stared at the sonar screen. Then suddenly, “Wait.”

Everyone turned toward him. The sonar image had changed. A strange pattern appeared beneath the boat. Not a natural rock formation. Not a reef. Something else. Sharan zoomed the display. The shape became clearer: lines, angles, structures.

Sharan whispered slowly, “That… doesn’t look natural.”

Joseph leaned closer. The sonar outline showed something massive resting on the ocean floor. Large rectangular forms. Broken sections. Rows that looked almost organized. Joseph exhaled quietly. “Those are structures.”

Keerthi blinked in surprise. “Buildings?”

Sharan nodded slowly. “Or ruins.”

Harish felt a wave of excitement rush through him. “So the island really existed…”

Abinaya looked down at the ocean surface. “And now it’s down there.”

Joseph straightened. “Time to confirm.”

Within minutes, the boat deck transformed into a preparation area. Scuba tanks were lifted from storage. Diving suits were pulled on. Oxygen regulators clicked into place. Joseph checked each piece of equipment with the calm precision of someone who had done this many times before.

“Remember,” he said firmly. “We stay together.”

Harish nodded. “Maximum depth?”

Joseph checked the sonar again. “About sixty-eight meters.”

Sharan raised his eyebrows. “That’s deep.”

Joseph nodded. “Very deep for scuba.”

Abinaya adjusted her diving mask. “We’ll move carefully.”

Sharan crossed his arms. “Keerthi and I will stay on the boat.”

Keerthi nodded. “We’ll monitor your oxygen levels and sonar signals.”

Joseph handed them the communication headset connected to the underwater radio system. “If anything looks wrong, call us back immediately.”

Sharan gave a confident nod. “Got it.”

Harish clipped the underwater light to his diving suit. Joseph looked at the group one final time. “Ready?”

Abinaya smiled slightly. “Let’s go find history.”

The ocean swallowed them with a quiet splash. Cold water wrapped around them instantly as the three divers began descending. Sunlight shimmered through the surface above them. Tiny bubbles floated upward as they slowly moved deeper.

Harish felt the familiar calm of scuba diving settle over him. Breathing through the regulator. Slow. Steady. Controlled.

Joseph signaled with his hand: *Follow me.*

They descended carefully. Ten meters. Twenty meters. Thirty meters. The sunlight began fading behind them. Their underwater lights switched on, cutting bright beams through the deep blue water. Fish scattered away from the strange lights.

Forty meters. Fifty meters. The pressure of the ocean began pressing harder against their bodies. Breathing became slightly heavier. Sharan’s voice crackled faintly through the underwater communication system. “You’re passing fifty meters.”

Joseph responded calmly. “Still descending.”

Sixty meters. The ocean here felt darker, quieter, almost empty. Then something strange happened. Harish felt a slight pull. Not strong, but noticeable. As if the water itself was guiding them downward. Abinaya noticed it too. She looked at Joseph. Joseph nodded slowly; he felt it as well. A faint current… almost like a field drawing them deeper.

Then, at sixty-eight meters, their lights finally reached the ocean floor. And Harish froze.

Below them, rising from the seabed like the ghost of a forgotten world, were ruins. Massive stone structures. Broken walls. Collapsed pillars. Entire streets buried beneath sand and coral.

Joseph signaled them to slow down. The pressure at this depth was intense. Human divers could not move too quickly here. They carefully reached the ocean floor. Fine sand rose gently around their feet. Abinaya turned slowly, shining her light across the area. Everywhere she looked: ruins, stone platforms, and ancient carved blocks.

Sharan’s amazed voice reached them through the radio. “We’re seeing the camera feed. That’s definitely a city.”

Harish swam forward slowly. Between many broken pillars and collapsed buildings, something larger stood in the distance. Something far bigger than the other structures. Joseph noticed it too. He directed his light toward it.

The beams revealed a massive shape rising from the ruins. A structure with towering pillars. A wide stone entrance. A carved roofline partly buried in sand and coral. It looked unmistakable. A temple.

Even after centuries underwater, the structure still stood stronger than the rest of the ruins around it. And strangely, the faint pulling sensation in the water seemed stronger in that direction. Abinaya looked at Joseph again. Even without speaking, they both understood. Whatever had drawn them here was inside that structure.

Joseph raised his hand and signaled forward: *We go there.*

The three divers began swimming cautiously through the ruins. Broken columns lay scattered across the ocean floor. Fragments of ancient carvings appeared beneath layers of coral. History surrounded them on every side.

But ahead, the ancient temple waited. Silent. Dark. And somehow… still calling them closer.
