# Chapter 11: The Awakening of the Temple

The chamber stood silent. At the center of the hall, the stone pedestal waited. Beneath the box, the circular hollow slot stared upward like an empty eye waiting for its key.

Harish looked at Abinaya. She slowly opened the pouch hanging from her belt. Inside it lay the Maragatham gemstone. Even in the dim temple light, the emerald glowed faintly.

Joseph stepped closer to the pedestal. “If this is the key,” he said quietly, “then the temple has been waiting for it for centuries.”

Abinaya carefully lifted the gemstone. For a moment she hesitated. Then she slowly placed it into the hollow slot beneath the box. The fit was perfect.

The moment the gemstone touched the stone, a deep vibration echoed through the chamber. Ancient gears hidden inside the pedestal began turning. Stone grinding against stone. The entire hall trembled slightly.

Harish stepped back. “It’s working.”

The pedestal slowly rotated. Then—*click*. The lock released. Joseph lifted the lid of the stone box carefully. Inside rested an ancient book. Thick and beautifully preserved, the cover was made of dark material bound with golden Tamil letters. Across its surface was written a single name: **Kadalkol**.

For a moment, none of them spoke. They had finally found it—the lost book written by King Athiyan himself. 

But that wasn’t all. Beside the book lay a tightly rolled parchment scroll. Joseph carefully lifted both items. “These must have been sealed together.”

Abinaya immediately opened her waterproof exploration bag. “Give them to me.” Joseph handed them over. She wrapped the book and the scroll carefully in waterproof layers and secured them inside the bag.

But the moment the book and the scroll were removed, something unexpected happened. The pedestal vibrated again. Harish looked down. The slot beneath the box slowly opened. A small circular stone plate rose upward from the hollow space. And resting on it was the Maragatham gemstone.

The temple had returned it. Abinaya quickly picked it up. “It gave the gemstone back to us.”

Joseph frowned slightly. “That means it wasn’t just a key.”

Harish looked at the glowing emerald. “It’s probably meant for something else.”

Abinaya carefully placed the gemstone back into her pouch. “Then we keep it safe.”

But the moment the pedestal completed its movement, a loud rumbling sound shook the entire temple. The floor trembled violently. Joseph looked up. “That doesn’t sound good.”

Then suddenly, a sharp hiss echoed through the chamber. Harish felt the air around them changing. He checked the small oxygen meter on his belt. “The oxygen level is dropping.”

Abinaya’s eyes widened. “The air chamber is failing!”

Joseph looked toward the entrance corridor. Water began leaking from cracks in the walls—thin streams at first, then stronger. The ancient protection system that had kept the temple dry for centuries was collapsing. 

And worse, the defense system had awakened again. One by one, heavy stone doors began slamming shut throughout the temple. 

*Boom. Boom. Boom.* Each corridor was sealing itself off. Harish grabbed his diving mask. “We have to move!”

Water rushed faster across the floor. Joseph scanned the chamber quickly. Then he spotted something. “Over there!” At the far side of the hall, a single stone door remained open: a narrow emergency passage. Ancient architects had designed it as a final escape route.

“Go!” Joseph shouted.

The three ran through the corridor. Behind them, the chamber began filling with ocean water. The sound of rushing waves echoed through the temple halls. Stone doors continued sealing behind them, but the emergency passage remained open. 

They sprinted down the corridor until suddenly the temple entrance appeared ahead—the same doorway where they had left their scuba diving equipment earlier. Water was already flooding into the entrance chamber. Harish quickly grabbed his oxygen tank. “Suit up!”

Within seconds they put their scuba masks back on. Joseph checked his pressure gauge. “Let’s go!”

They plunged back into the ocean. Cold water swallowed them instantly. Above them, faint sunlight shimmered far away. Joseph activated the underwater communication signal. “Sharan, do you copy?”

The radio crackled, then Sharan’s voice burst through clearly. “Joseph! We see you on sonar!”

Keerthi’s voice followed quickly. “We’re ready to pull you up!”

Joseph signaled to Harish and Abinaya: *Ascend.*

They began rising slowly through the deep blue water. Sixty meters. Forty meters. The light from the surface grew brighter. Above them, the faint shape of their boat appeared through the water. Sharan and Keerthi stood at the edge of the deck, watching the sonar screen. 

Then suddenly, three figures appeared beneath the boat. “They’re here!” Keerthi shouted.

Joseph reached the surface first, then Harish, then Abinaya. Sharan quickly threw down the safety line. One by one, they climbed onto the deck. The moment their feet touched the boat floor, everyone collapsed in exhaustion.

Sharan laughed in relief. “You scared us down there.”

Keerthi looked at Abinaya’s bag. “Did you get it?”

Abinaya slowly opened the waterproof compartment. Inside, the ancient book rested safely. She lifted it carefully: **Kadalkol**.

Joseph smiled quietly. “Looks like the king’s story survived.”

But none of them yet knew that the Maragatham gemstone now in Abinaya’s pouch still held another secret waiting to be revealed.
