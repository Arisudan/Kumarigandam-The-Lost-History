# Chapter 5: The Map in the Middle

The underground library had grown quieter. The thick book about King Athiyan still lay open on the stone table. Beside it rested the fragile map fragment Joseph had picked up from its pages.

Everyone gathered around the table. The lantern light flickered across the parchment. Harish leaned closer. “What does it show?”

The fragment was small, barely the size of Joseph’s palm. Much of its edges were torn away long ago. The ink had faded with time, yet some lines remained clear enough to follow. Sharan tilted the map toward the lantern. “There’s water everywhere around this area.”

Joseph studied it carefully. “Yes… this is definitely ocean.”

Abinaya traced a faint curved line on the map. “Look here.”

A dotted chain of shapes stretched across the page. Small land formations connected in a line. Joseph nodded slowly. “That looks like Ram Setu.”

Keerthi’s eyes widened. “The bridge of islands between India and Sri Lanka?”

Joseph nodded again. “Yes.” He pointed toward another mark further below. “And this part here…” He paused. “…looks like the western coast of Sri Lanka.”

Sharan exhaled softly. “So this fragment is showing a part of the sea between those places.”

Abinaya leaned even closer. Near the center of the fragment was a tiny circle drawn in darker ink. Inside it, a single marking. “What is that?” Keerthi asked.

Joseph rubbed his chin. “An island.”

Everyone looked up. “But it’s not marked on any modern map,” Sharan added.

The priest stepped closer to the table. “That is because many islands that existed in ancient times have long disappeared.”

Harish studied the tiny circle again. According to the drawing, the island was small. Very small. Surrounded entirely by deep ocean. The sketch showed a ring of land… and in the center, something that looked like trees.

“A forest?” Keerthi asked.

Abinaya nodded. “A small island with a forest in the middle.”

Joseph frowned. “But the fragment is incomplete.” He turned the parchment over. “The rest of the map is missing.”

Everyone fell silent. The fragment showed a place. But not enough to fully understand it. Harish leaned back slightly. “So we know there was an island somewhere near Ram Setu and the western side of Sri Lanka.” He gestured toward the torn edges. “But we don’t know how big it was… or what exactly was there.”

Confusion settled over the group. The clue felt important. But incomplete. The priest watched them quietly. Then he spoke. “You must understand something.”

Everyone turned toward him. “The story of King Athiyan did not disappear by accident.”

Harish frowned. “What do you mean?”

The priest folded his hands behind his back and looked at the old book again. “Long ago, when the southern lands began to fall… many histories were intentionally hidden.”

Abinaya listened closely. “Hidden?”

“Yes.” The priest turned a few pages carefully. “Some kings believed their knowledge would be safer if it vanished from the world.”

Joseph spoke softly. “Because of war?”

The priest nodded. “Yes.” He pointed to a passage in the book. “In the final years of Athiyan’s rule, the coastal territories faced great conflicts.”

Sharan crossed his arms. “Enemies?”

“Many,” the priest replied. “Rival kingdoms… invading forces… and disasters that reshaped the sea itself.”

Harish glanced at the map fragment again. “So Athiyan hid something?”

The priest’s expression grew serious. “That is what many ancient texts suggest.”

Joseph suddenly straightened. A thought flashed through his mind. “Athiyan ruled the Seven Eastern Coastal Territories, right?”

The priest nodded. “Yes.”

Joseph pointed to the map. “Those territories would have had coastal landmarks.” Everyone looked at him. Joseph continued thinking aloud. “Ports. Navigation points. Trade routes.” His eyes moved across the map again. “If Athiyan wanted to hide something important…” He tapped the island marking. “…he would choose a place far from the main coastal routes.”

Sharan’s eyes lit up. “An isolated island.”

Joseph nodded slowly. “Exactly.”

The priest looked thoughtful. “That is possible.” He walked toward another section of the underground library. Rows of ancient records filled the shelves. “I believe there may be another document about the final years of Athiyan and Queen Mathi.”

Abinaya watched eagerly as the priest began searching. Dust-covered scrolls moved aside. Old record bundles were carefully opened. Minutes passed. Then suddenly—

“Here.”

The priest lifted a thin but well-preserved manuscript. “This record speaks about the final messages of the royal family.” He brought it to the table and opened it gently. The writing inside was faded but still readable. The priest began translating slowly. “This passage describes a farewell…”

Joseph leaned closer. “A farewell?”

The priest nodded. “Yes.” He read aloud. “Before leaving for war, King Athiyan gave a final message to Queen Mathi.”

The friends exchanged glances. Harish slowly reached into Abinaya’s bag and brought out the Maragatham gemstone. The priest’s eyes widened again as he looked at the inscription. He compared it to the manuscript. Then he spoke quietly. “This is it.”

Everyone leaned forward. “The gemstone you found…” He paused. “…is the same one mentioned in this record.”

Abinaya’s voice barely rose above a whisper. “The last message?”

The priest nodded. “Yes.” He translated the line from the manuscript. “Before leaving for battle, King Athiyan carved a message for Mathi into a Maragatham stone.”

Joseph felt a chill run down his spine. Harish looked at the gemstone again. “For you… my love.” The words suddenly carried much deeper meaning.

Keerthi spoke softly. “So this wasn’t just a love gift.”

The priest shook his head. “No.” He pointed toward another line in the manuscript. “This message was also a farewell.”

The room fell silent. “Athiyan believed he might never return from the war.”

Abinaya imagined the moment. A king preparing for battle. A queen watching him leave. A single gemstone holding his final words. The priest turned one more page. “This passage also says something else.”

Everyone looked up again. “Before leaving, Athiyan ensured that the history of his people would not vanish.”

Joseph frowned. “How?”

The priest looked toward the map fragment on the table. “He hid the records.”

Sharan exhaled slowly. “In that location.”

The priest nodded. “Yes.”

Harish felt excitement rising in his chest again. “That island.”

The priest closed the manuscript carefully. “Athiyan gathered his knowledge, records, and history… and preserved them in a single hidden place.” He tapped the map fragment gently. “That place may be here.”

The lantern flame flickered as the group stared at the tiny island drawn on the parchment. Somewhere between Ram Setu and the western waters of Sri Lanka… a forgotten location waited. A place where the last king of a lost land might have hidden the history of an entire civilization.

Harish held the gemstone tightly. The message now felt alive. A voice from centuries ago. And somehow—it had reached them.

But the Maragatham stone still held more secrets.
