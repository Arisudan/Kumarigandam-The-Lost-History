# Chapter 9: The Temple of the Silent King

The ancient temple stood before them like a giant shadow in the deep sea. Even after centuries beneath the ocean, its structure remained powerful. Massive stone pillars rose from the seabed, their surfaces covered with coral and sea moss. Broken statues lay scattered around the entrance, their shapes worn by time and water.

Harish shone his diving light toward the temple door. The entrance was enormous. Two towering stone doors stood half buried in sand.

Joseph swam closer. As they approached, something strange began to happen. The crushing pressure of the deep sea that had been tightening around their bodies began to ease. Harish noticed it first. He flexed his fingers inside the diving gloves. The pressure that had made every movement heavy was slowly fading.

Abinaya looked around in surprise. The water here felt different. Almost calm. Almost protected. Joseph studied the area carefully. “It’s like some kind of barrier,” his voice crackled through the underwater radio.

The three divers slowly reached the front of the temple. Harish turned his light to the right side of the door and froze. Carved into the stone wall beside the entrance was a symbol. A royal mark. Abinaya immediately recognized it. Her eyes widened behind the diving mask. She quickly opened the waterproof case attached to her belt and took out the small copied page from the Athiyan history book she had brought from the temple library. 

She held it beside the carving. It matched perfectly. “The royal symbol of King Athiyan,” she whispered.

Joseph nodded slowly. “That confirms it.”

Harish looked back at the temple doors. “This is the place.”

Abinaya’s voice trembled with excitement. “The book Kadalkol must be here.”

Joseph moved closer to the door. Along the edge of the entrance, he noticed something unusual: a stone lever mechanism. Ancient, but still intact. He brushed away layers of coral and sand. “Looks like a spring lock,” he said.

Harish swam beside him. “You think it still works?”

Joseph gave a small smile. “There’s only one way to find out.”

He pulled the stone lever. For a moment, nothing happened. Then suddenly, a deep grinding sound echoed through the water. The massive doors slowly began to move. Sand clouds rose from the ocean floor as the ancient mechanism pushed the doors apart.

As the gap opened wider, a rush of air burst outward from inside the temple. Harish’s eyes widened. Air. Actual air. Joseph immediately checked his oxygen meter. Then he looked toward the open chamber. Inside the temple, there was no water. The space beyond the door was dry.

Abinaya stared in disbelief. “A preserved air chamber… after all these centuries?”

Joseph removed his diving mask cautiously and stepped inside the entrance. He took a careful breath. Fresh air filled his lungs. He laughed softly. “It’s breathable.”

Harish and Abinaya followed. One by one they removed their diving masks and oxygen tanks. The ancient chamber was dimly lit by small openings in the ceiling where faint filtered light reached inside. The walls were made of polished stone. Carvings filled every surface: scenes of ships, kings, battles, and large gatherings of people.

Harish whispered in amazement, “This place has been sealed for centuries.”

Joseph looked around carefully. “The temple must have been built with a pressure barrier to keep water out.”

Abinaya gently touched one of the wall carvings. “These are records… history carved in stone.”

The chamber extended deeper inside. But after walking a short distance, they suddenly stopped. The path split. Two long corridors stretched in opposite directions: one to the left, and one to the right.

Joseph frowned. “Well…”

Harish crossed his arms. “That complicates things.”

Abinaya studied both hallways. Each looked equally ancient and equally mysterious. “We don’t know which path leads to the chamber with Kadalkol,” she said.

Joseph nodded slowly. “If we explore together, it might take too long.”

Harish thought for a moment. Then he spoke. “We split.”

Joseph looked at him. “Two paths. Two teams.”

Abinaya hesitated slightly. “But there are dangers we don’t know about.”

Joseph placed a reassuring hand on the stone wall. “We stay alert.”

Harish nodded. “I’ll go with Abinaya.”

Joseph smiled slightly. “Then I’ll take the other path.”

The plan was decided. Abinaya and Harish turned toward the left corridor. Joseph walked toward the right passage alone. Before separating, they exchanged one last look. “Stay safe,” Harish said. Joseph nodded. “You too.” Then they went their separate ways.

### Harish and Abinaya’s Path

The corridor stretched long and narrow. Their footsteps echoed softly against the stone floor. Ancient torches lined the walls, though none had been lit for centuries. Abinaya ran her fingers along the carvings as they walked. “These walls tell stories,” she said quietly.

Harish shone his light ahead. Suddenly, the floor beneath them shifted. “Stop!” Harish pulled Abinaya back just in time. A section of the stone floor suddenly collapsed. Blocks of rock fell into a deep dark pit below.

Abinaya gasped. “That was a trap.”

Harish nodded. “A defense mechanism.” He carefully tested the ground ahead using a loose stone. Several tiles immediately dropped into the pit. They had to move carefully, step by step, slowly navigating through the ancient trap corridor.

But the deeper they went, the darker the passage became. And somewhere ahead, they could hear a faint mechanical sound.

### Joseph’s Path

Joseph’s corridor felt colder. The air was heavier here. He walked carefully, shining his light across the walls. Unlike the other corridor, this one had enormous statues standing along both sides: warriors, guards, and protectors of the temple. Their stone faces looked almost alive in the dim light.

Joseph continued forward. Then suddenly, a deep rumbling sound echoed behind him. He turned around. The entrance door of the corridor had sealed shut. Joseph frowned. “Well… that’s not good.”

As he turned back, one of the giant stone statues moved. Just slightly. Joseph froze. Then the statue’s eyes began to glow faintly. Ancient defense guardians had awakened.

Joseph slowly stepped backward. “Okay…” He whispered to himself. “This temple really doesn’t want visitors.”

The stone guardians began moving, heavy stone feet scraping against the floor. Joseph looked around quickly. He needed to find a way through before the ancient protectors reached him.

Deep within the temple, two paths had been chosen. Two dangers had awakened. And somewhere in the hidden chambers of this ancient place, the lost book Kadalkol waited. But reaching it would not be easy.
